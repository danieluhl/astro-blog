I grew up watching SNL and I've watched _a lot_ of SNL. My favorite sketch, the
one I re-watch the most, is David Pumpkins.

If you haven't seen it, it's a haunted elevator sketch done during Halloween.
100 Floors of frights. There are a variety of scary floors, the doors open and
it's scary. Then at one floor they meet David Pumpkins. A man dressed in a
pumpkin suit with two skeleton B-boys dancing beside him. The attendants are
more confused than scared. David Pumpkins ends with, "Any questions?" and the
attendants reply, "yes, so many!".

It's a good joke on it's own but I bring it up for one part that I think of
often. At one point the attendants start asking questions and one says, "and the
b-boys are...", then both b-boy skeletons reply in unison, "part of it!"

What is happening? _this_

Why is it happening? _it just is_

And this particular thing? _PART OF IT!_

Anything you can pay attention to is worthy of your attention. All parts of
experience, you can pay attention to. The nature of consciousness is completely
unrestricted, open. When you feel pain, the mind creates aversion but the
aversion is to the already open experience of pain. When we see a beautiful
sunset the mind wants to create clinging, but we already are fully open to the
experience of the sunset.

I picked up Brandon Sanderson's Stormlight Archive and made it to the third book
before deciding it's not worth it. The book was in a bit of a rut. It's not
comfortable or pleasant to read. And I knew from the previous works, that there
may not be some big payoff from this discomfort (not soon at least). So why
would I spend my time and attention with something like this? I have better
things to do. I tell myself it's boring, or just not for me, and move on.

Six years later I picked it back up and finished the series. And now I
understand. The books would be not even half as good if they didn't have the
difficult parts. The pain, drama, boredom, uncomfortable subjects - part of it!
It makes the books more real. It makes the morales and lessons pop out because
they directly correspond to my life experience, and beyond.

So when I feel pain in my back or neck when I wake up, I let the experience flow
with open acceptance just as much as the beautiful sunset. Because both are
equally worthy of my attention.

And the pain is... part of it!
And the sunset is... part of it!
And the frustration is... part of it!
And the noticing is.. part of it!
