Hey man, how you doin?

> Yo, not bad, my car is broken tho

Oh, I'm sorry, what happened?

> Just hit a pothole and the tire blew out. I have a couple mechanics looking at
it... stupid car can't do anything, it's gotten so worn down and old

Yeah, these things require upkeep, you gotta give it a tune-up once in a while

> Ugh, it should just work! Anyways, one mechanic says he will need to charge me
more to fix it. I already pay registration and insurance, I'm not paying more
for a broken tire that's not even my fault.

Well I guess you don't really have a choice right? You kind of have the car you
have and better to fix it up and be able to drive.

> Nah, the other mechanic has a much better plan. He will charge less and really
put it to that crappy car. He'll smash out the other tires, such a broken car.

Wait what!? But then you won't be able to drive? Won't that cost more in the
long run.

> The car is fundamentally broken, may as well smash it up if it's not working for
me.

I mean... it was working for you even if you didn't see it. If you'd just repair
the tire, the rest of the car does work pretty well.

> Not for long, we're throwing out the transmission

What! Why would you do that? It wasn't the best but at least it worked. That's
going to be a total pain to repair. Your car may never work again!

> Good. I want to go back to the 1920s when the founding car makers really knew
how to make a car. No fancy computers or gadgets, just good ol' american cars!

Ok, hold up, you're not getting a new car or an old car because the mechanic is
just pocketing all your money and buying himself a lambo. Cars in the 20s
sucked. We know a lot more about cars now and you'd be miserable in a 20s car.

> Meh, this car is not working for me. I always hated it. Burn it down and bring
me back to the old Ford.

But how will you get groceries and get your kids to school and get to work? None
of this makes sense!

> I'll just Uber, better to pay private industry than repair an old car

Won't that be way more expensive and less efficient for everyone?

> Meh. I'm sure we'll have universal basic car rides powered by AI or something.
I try not to think about it too much, just do what feels right. Now grab a bat
and help be bash out the windshield.
