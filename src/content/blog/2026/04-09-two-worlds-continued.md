There is no such thing as money, it's just an idea and some paper. There's no
such thing as trees. What is the genetic difference between a pine and a maple.
What about a palm tree. More of a big grass, do we need another idea for "big
grass"?

Ideas live in one world, but they are still "real", in so far as they are
useful. And they have more concrete underpinnings. The evolution of social
reciprocity is baked into the way we value and move money in a meritocratic way.
Our evolution is baked into how we value money and so there is some thread of
reality under the idea. Ideas are so powerful in this way.

Likewise we can see the genetic evolution of the underpinnings of most
religions. Good religions are good humanist values. Rational materialist minds
come to the same conclusions. Some religions are like mental shortcuts to
understand the underlying moral framework that any civilized society will
naturally come to given our evolved nature as humanity.

But if this is true, and our human values are baked in, then consider Jihadism
or imagine some other culture that indoctrinates its children into some extreme
belief. If our moral compass comes from our society, then what if our world
simply had a different society built on different ideas. For example, if we
believe that half the population should never drive cars, should not take
certain jobs, and should focus on raising many children. This may give an
evolutionary advantage to such a society and so those practices may become
similarly "baked in" to the genetic evolution of such a society, and over time
we may see this as an obvious moral stance for any human to take.

So we need better underpinnings of moral frameworks, even those built into our
DNA. In the same way that physics and mathematics has quickly outpaced our
evolutionary abilities, we need to embrace a higher point of view, and loop that
back to become the underpinning moral framework for our religions, ideas,
beliefs, etc.

One way to do this is to embrace consequentialist utilitarianism. That is,
actions have consequences, and we should aim to optimize the most good for the
most conscious creatures, and minimize pain and suffering for the most conscious
creatures. Doing this is good, failing to do this is evil.

## Part 2 Doing good things

There are no "good" or "bad" people in the world as our mind imagines there
should be. Building hate day after day for your neighbor based on your 2 minutes
per month of brief judgemental interaction is building a world in which a person
is innately evil. You may seeth over this person, sending hate and anger in your
mind over and over and over until there's no doubt that you would do all sorts
of violence to this deep evil that you imagine lives near you, but really only
lives in your head.

There are evil acts in the world. People do bad things. But in the way our minds
invent, in the idea, those people are never actually _that_. They are not "bad"
or "good" in the way our mind creates and machinates on the ideas of good and
bad. In the same way, you do good and bad things throughout the day, but you are
too complex, too open and wandering, to ever be truly labeled as one or the
other. The best you could say is that, all things considered as far as I know,
I have done more good deeds than bad.

Consider Hitler. He had excellent policies on animal rights. His policies on
dogs are still used in Germany today and are still somewhat progressive in the
world. His situation was extremely difficult. He, like all of us, was largely
shaped by his changing context throughout his life. His choices had
consequences, but the best we can say is that, all things considered, he did
many more evil deeds than good, and so it was a failing of the world as to not
better constrain him and his ideas.

Meta practices the opposite and can be a good way to cultivate a more gentle,
better perspective on the world. The reality is that most humans do a pretty
good job at acting in a good way (as good is defined above). And so they deserve
our good wishes. Wishing others well cultivates a beautiful garden in the mind,
which in turn makes the world a more pleasant place to live. I'm sure there are
cases of people taking meta too far, consult a psychologist if this is you,
because it is after all the same hallucination in the opposite direction.
