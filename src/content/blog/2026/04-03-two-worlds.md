I straddle a window sill of my childhood home.

I look to the left and see the open world. The hills, the trees, the grass, and
a beautiful sunset. Open sky as far as I can see. Cars pass occasionally on the
country road. The neighbor is upset with his son. Life carries on as it is;
beautiful, grusome, painful, blissful, but always true to itself.

I turn my head and look to the right and see the inside of the house. The room is small and cozy. Wood walls and
floor, white ceiling, well lit. There is a decorative banzai tree that I spend
careful time maintaining. There's a small wood stove that smells good and looks
good but is rarely used. The TV does get a lot of use, as does the video games. The room is not overly glamorous, it's well lived in
and over the years has been shaped around to fit me like a glove. It holds my
memories. Sneaking out at night to see a movie, bringing my girlfriend home and
closing the door when that was against the rules, hitting record at just the
right time so I can record my favorite songs off the radio onto my cassette
player. Playing legos and batman with my brother. So many video games.

I slowly turn my head back and face the wall directly. I let my vision blur,
taking everything in at once. The open world, sky, trees, and the cozy room,
memories, hopes, worries. Both worlds are real, alive, lovely to exist in. Both
worlds are full of sorrow and pain and joy and pleasure. But my room is mine,
and the wide world is for everyone. When I bring suffering into my room, I bear
it alone. When the world is suffering, we seek to help each other out. In my
room, I have no free will, there are only consequences arising from prior
conditions. But in the world, I am me, and you are you, and we must get on in
complex interpersonal relationships. In my room I'm free to dream and build any
world I want. I can play with angels and daemons and knights and fairies. But in
the open world we can only abide by what reality tells us and we are constantly
trying to not be fooled by ourselves.

I love my room. I know others who do not. I have a friend who keeps a rabid dog
in his room. He hates that dog and they both try to kill each other every day.
The room is a mess, cramped, too small, and yet he barely ever leaves his room
to go outside. He's embarrassed of his room and doesn't want anyone to see what
hell he's been living through. I want to help, I want to get him out into the
world and show him true beauty. I want him to have hope that he can clean up his
room and make things right and over time he too can love his room like I love
mine. But his room is trapping him. His window gets smaller every day and as it
fades, so does his hope of escape. I want to shove my hand through his small
window and pull him out, but this is just a self serving fantasy, just another
small joy in my wonderful room, I can't know what it's like in his room any more
than he can know what it's like in mine.

Over time, my room fades. I get new lights, paint the walls, update my
technology, and piece by piece, it's a new room, unrecognizable from the one of
my childhood. Some memories live in the walls, but the light is different, and
so they can never be seen the same way anyways. There's a big hole in one of the
walls. That I don't think will ever get fully patched as it went too deep. When
I look into that hole I feel all the deep feelings I had when I made that hole.
At the bottom of the hole lives a small lego castle I made. Complete with all my
hopes and dreams and desires and ideas for what I thought I might share with
another, but turned out to only be a toy in my head, never really constructed in
the outside world no matter how much I tried to push it.

I sit in the window sill. I tilt my head from side to side. All of experience.
So many contradictions. Trees on one side, wood on the other. Sun on one side,
artificial lights on the other. What is real? What is real? What is really real?
My room is constructed. The wide open world is there, seen from my window,
flooding into my eyes, constructed to make sense. My room doesn't always make
sense, but that doesn't make it less real.

My mother always told me I need to take care of my toys. They are precious and
important to me, but not so much to others in the outside world. If you bring a
toy outside, it definitely won't last long. The sun will make it fade, the dirt
will gobble it up, someone will throw it in the trash or crush it. But I've seen
some other kids bring out their toy. They bring it out and show it and say the
most amazing things about their toy. They make all the kids want that toy. I
know better, it's obvious what it is, that it will fade in the open world, that
it's theirs and nobody else can really have it. It's dangerous bringing toys out
like that. Some things should be kept safely in your room. You can have others
over to play in your room, that can be great fun, but don't invite the entire
neighborhood at once, and don't expect everyone to like the same toys you like.
